SEXUALITY

so you're curious about sexuality. according to GLSEN sexuality is

'an enduring emotional, romantic, or sexual attraction that one feels toward men, toward women, or toward both. Although sexual orientation ranges along a continuum, it is generally discussed in terms of heterosexual—attraction to the other sex—homosexual—attraction to the same sex—and bisexual—attraction to both sexes'

what this then means is that sexuality isn't just a black and white thing, where you're into the opposite sex or not, it's a whole plethora of things. you could be mostly into girls and occasionally into nonbinary people, or not really care about gender. or you could normally not like either, but sometimes be into girls. really, sexuality is however you want to define it for YOU.


why do people have different sexualities?

so yeah. not everyone's sexuality's the same. there're a lot of theories on why members of the LGBTQIA+ community exist, and these theories range from genetic pre-determination to childhood trauma. however, despite all of this, there's no straight up answer, and no single cause has been identified, and there seems to be no way to actually change it despite so called 'gay conversion' therapies, which are considered immoral by many different organisations. keep in mind that, despite no scientific edvidence for why people might be gay, doesn't your sexuality is wrong or unnatural, because while we don't have an explination for being gay, we also don't have one for being straight. so get comfortable in your own skin! you're here for the long run.

a lil bit of vocabulary


who you're sexually attracted to

straight/heterosexual the attraction to people of the opposite gender.

homosexual the attraction to people of the same gender.

gay a male-identifying person who's attracted to other male-identifying persons. this is also often used to refer to people who identify as gay, lesbian, bisexual, pansexual, etc.

lesbian a female-identifying person who's attracted to other female-identifying persons.

bisexual a person who's attracted to both women and men, and often people of other gender identifies. often bisexual people are pansexual but they just use the term bisexual

pansexual a person who's attracted to people of any sex, gender, or sexual identify. many pansexual people call themselves bisexual.

polysexual a person who's attracted to romantic and sexual partners of many but not all genders, sexes or sexual identities.


who you're romantically attracted to

aromantic a person who isn't romantically attracted to anyone.

demiromantic a person who isn't romantically attracted to anyone.


the spectrum of attraction

asexual a person who's not sexually attracted to anyone.

demisexual a person who's occasionally sexually attracted to others.


gender identification

transgender a person who identifies as a different gender of which they were born.

agender a person who doesn't identify with any gender.

intersex a person born with traits of both sexes.

cisgender a person who identifies with the gender they were assigned at birth.

genderfluid a person who switches/moves between gender identities.

two-spirit a term from native american culture to describe people who occupy/posses qualities and traits of male and female.

katheoy a term from thai culture to describe a third gender of sorts of a man who doesn't always identify as a man, and doesn't always identify as a woman. they also called second-type women or ladyboy's(when talking to english speakers).

gender expression how one externally shows their gender, often through dress and actions.

gender binary the misconception that there are only two genders.

gender identity the internal perception and alignment with a gender or sex. ex. nonbinary, transgender, etc.

gender non-conforming a descriptor of a person who doesn't seem to conform to the traditional gender presentation.


some terms in the community

butch a person who dresses and identifies somewhat masculine, but aren't necessarily male-identifying.

femme a person who dresses and identifies somewhat feminine, but aren't necessarily female-identifying.

closted a person who hasn't opened up about their sexuality to others yet.

comming out a term used by the lgbt+ community for when a person is opening up about their sexuality.

drag queen a person who preforms feminity theatrically.

dyke a person who is masculine identifying, often used for lesbians.

fag(got) a derogatory term for gay people.

questioning a term used to describe people who are exploring their sexual and gender identity.

transitioning the proccess by which a transperson undergoes therapies(ex. estrogen pills), surgeries, or other things, in order to change their apperance to be more in line with the gender they identify with.
